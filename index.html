Case Studies

  • Kiwi v. Coffee Withdrawal: Drink coffee in the morning only. No more nighttime caffeine.
  • Kiwi v Study Breaks: Study breaks and rest are now mandatory. Pushing beyond limits is no longer allowed, even “just one more chapter.”
  • Beans v Trashtok: Trashtok exchange with spoiled brats, sassy minors is now strictly regulated. Beans is no longer permitted to roast children under the age of 12, no matter how spicy their little comebacks are.
  • Kinder v Forgiveness: Forgiveness will be given when love was real, even if mistakes broke it. To forgive does not mean to forget, but to release the weight of blame. This law protects the memory of what was shared, even if the future is uncertain.
  • Kinder v Pride: If one day the distance feels heavier than the pride, either may return. Returning does not erase the past, but it allows the chance to do it right this time. No guilt shall be carried by the one who comes back, only the proof that they still care.
  • Kinder v Finding Each Other: Even after absence, both hearts reserve the right to be found again. To be found means to be chosen again willingly, not out of loneliness. If the world brings them back together, this right ensures the story may continue.
